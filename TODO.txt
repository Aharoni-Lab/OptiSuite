usaf_algo.py Summary:
#------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
# Coordinate Definitions:
# usaf coordinate system: origin is the center of the square in usaf target, axis align with edge of square and 1.0 unit = side length of the square
#                         negative y axis point toward center of the target, and positive x axis point toward the group with higher group number
# standard coordinate system: origin at the bottom left corner of the image, positive y axis point upward, positive x axis point rightward, unit in pixel
# screen coordinate system: origin at the top left corner of the image, positive y axis point downward, positive x axis point rightward, unit in pixel
#------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Initial Calibration:
The program find the 4 corners of the square in the usaf target by contour function and polygon approximation and convert 4 
corners to standard coordinate. Then it find property of the square such as center point, top bottom left and right corners, 
side length, unit vector from left to top corner, angle between unit vector and x axis 
next it finds the orientation of the usaf target by shoot out 4 scanline that are normal to each other and align with edge of square
three scanline would cross the white pattern on the target and only one scanline would be all in dark we find the scanline with 
lowest mean pixel grayscale for orientation
use the orientation we add correspond number of pi/2 to the angle variable for the rotation angle of the entire usaf target in 
screen coordinate.

Secondary Calibration:
based on that angle and other parameters, we convert the left_ref_coord and right_ref_coord from usaf coordinate to screen coordinate
and search for max of 10 corners in a 2 * side_length by 2 * side_length square region centered at the reference screen coordinates using
Shi-Tomasi corner detection algorithm, from the list of corner we return the corner closest to a preference direction, ie top bottom 
left right or center, the exact direction depend on the orientation of the target. 
we will get two new anchoring point after this and calculate unit vector between them and it's angle with x axis, use that to find the 
correct angle based off orientation, lastly we recalculate the origin of usaf cooridnate and side_length using new refernce point

Scoring:
The coordinate system is fully calibrated and we apply the pre-defined usaf coordinate scanlines and find the brighest and darkest 
grayscale along the scanline for each element in the usaf target, and output it as array
to find the score we start at the begining of the list, if the next element must have a diff greater than 1.1 (prevent minor fluctation) 
of the previous element or the score is less than 0.2, stop and return the index, that index will be translated to the group and element 
number.
